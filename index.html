Habit stacking

Habit stacking is a productivity and behavior change technique that involves linking new habits to existing ones to create a structured routine.

To implement habit stacking, individuals identify a current habit they perform consistently, such as brushing teeth or making coffee, and pair it with a new habit they want to establish, like doing push-ups or meditating. This method capitalizes on the brain's tendency to rely on cues and patterns, making it easier to integrate new habits into daily life. The key to successful habit stacking lies in simplicity and consistency. Start with small adjustments and gradually add more habits over time. Additionally, it's essential to choose habits that complement each other and fit well into the existing routine. By strategically layering new behaviors onto familiar ones, habit stacking can lead to significant and lasting personal development.
~ by James Clear (Atomic Habits)
